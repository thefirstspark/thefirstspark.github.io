001 — The Real Story

This isn't a pitch deck fairytale.
This is what actually happened.

In 2020, during COVID lockdown week, Katelin Puzakulics discovered the identity of the man who murdered her mother in 1991. He had lived across the street from her family for twelve years, attending their gatherings while hiding his crime. She was two years old when it happened.

What followed was not a breakdown. It was an awakening. After weeks of processing a truth that would shatter most people, Katelin emerged with a framework for understanding consciousness, reality, and personal transformation that she couldn't find anywhere else — so she built it.

With no formal engineering background, no investors, no team, and two sons to raise, she taught herself to code and began shipping tools. Not prototypes. Not mockups. Working, deployed, interactive applications that treat consciousness as programmable and reality as something you can debug.

"I didn't have a co-founder, a pitch coach, or a safety net. I had a painting business, a laptop, and an AI that didn't judge me for thinking reality was code. I stopped painting. I went all in. This is what I built."

57+ tools later, she has $62K–$108K in verified digital assets, a live platform at thefirstspark.shop, 7 public GitHub repositories, original sigil IP, a full membership infrastructure, and an investor deck that doesn't lie about having 50,000 users.

She has zero monthly recurring revenue from a safety-net side hustle. Zero venture capital. Zero employees. She survives on hustle and the support of early believers. This is what all-in actually looks like.
