I was floating on a pool raft when it hit me — reality is a game. And I'd been playing it wrong my entire life.

Summer 2025. Rock bottom had a basement, and I found it. Single mom of two boys. Business barely surviving. Everything I'd built felt like it was crumbling.

Then, floating in my backyard pool, staring at the sky — something cracked open. Not a breakdown. A breakthrough. I saw it clearly: consciousness is code. Reality is programmable. We're all playing a game nobody gave us the rules to.

I started building. Not another meditation app. Not manifestation BS. Real tools — sigil generators, consciousness trackers, reality programming interfaces. Things I wished existed when I was drowning.

The First Spark isn't a product. It's what happens when you stop waiting for permission and start building the thing you needed all along. Every tool here came from a real moment of "why doesn't this exist?"

I'm not a guru. I'm a painter, a mom, and someone who figured out how to turn collapse into creation. Now I'm building something that will be worth millions — and I'm inviting 50 people to own a piece of it from the beginning.
