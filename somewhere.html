Before the spark · 1991 — 2019

The Fracture

In 1991, my mother Rachael was murdered. For nearly three decades, that wound sat open — unsolved, unclosed, inherited. I grew up inside a cold case. I learned early that reality does not always tell you the truth on its own. Sometimes you have to read the code yourself.

I was not a tech founder on a glide path. I was a single mother in Akron, Ohio, building survival systems out of necessity — paint jobs, side hustles, whatever kept the lights on while I raised two kids and carried a story the world had not yet heard.

The ignition · 2020

The First Spark

In 2020, everything broke open. I solved my mother's 29-year cold case. The killer had been living across the street. Investigation Discovery ran the story. The fracture that had defined my life finally had a name, a face, a verdict.

You do not build consciousness technology from comfort. You build it from the place where the old reality stops working.

That year I also had an awakening — not a brand story, a lived one. I saw that consciousness interfaces with reality the way code interfaces with a machine. Patterns underneath. Timelines branching. Inherited programs running in the bloodline. And I started building tools to help people see it, select it, and debug it.

The First Spark was not born in a boardroom. It was born on a raft in the middle of a storm, with no plan B and no safety net — only the refusal to accept the reality I'd been handed.

The build · 2020 — 2024

Teaching Myself to Code the Invisible

I taught myself to program by building what I needed. Not tutorials for their own sake — interfaces for transformation. Sacred geometry trainers. Collapse protocols. Timeline navigators. Lineage debuggers. Soul maps that weave numerology, astrology, and the questions a person is actually carrying.

Every tool was a hypothesis made touchable: reality is programmable, consciousness is the code. I published the Selector Model thesis. I wrote a 75,000-word novel. I shipped page after page while parenting, while grieving, while proving that a solo founder with a fracture for a foundation can outbuild teams with funding.

The platform · 2024 — 2025

From Tools to Infrastructure

The experiments became a platform. Soul Maps went live — $22, hand-decoded, delivered in minutes, with 12 months of updates. The Sparkverse opened on Whop: free lobby, paid lounge, lifetime OG tier. Affiliates started earning 35% per map. Press found us — Voyage Ohio, Investigation Discovery, Paula Zahn.

I automated the pipeline: checkout → decode → deploy → deliver. No inventory. No warehouse. No extraction model. Reciprocity, not surveillance. People pay for value. They keep what they buy. The community grows because the tools actually work.
