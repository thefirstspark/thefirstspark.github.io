Operations Playbook

The Sell Protocol

90-Day Daily Ops Plan + Video Playbook for The First Spark

The Daily Rhythm

Same structure every day. Variations come from the weekly theme. This is the machine.

Morning Block — 30 min (before anything else)
  • 1 video recorded. 60-90 seconds max. Phone, natural light, no script — just the talking points from the Video Playbook tab. Post to X and TikTok.
  • 1 X post. A thought, a hot take, a question. Not a promo. Something someone wants to reply to or repost.
  • Check DMs. Reply to every single one. Conversations convert. Funnels don't.
Midday Block — 90 min (the money hours)
  • Soul Map work. Either (a) build a client's map, (b) create a public demo Soul Map to post as content, or (c) do outreach to someone who might want one.
  • 3 outbound DMs or comments. Not spam. Find people talking about consciousness, astrology, self-discovery, simulation theory — engage genuinely, then mention what you do when it's natural.
  • 1 piece of ecosystem content. Drop something in the Sparkverse Whop community, even if it's just a prompt or a thought. Keep the space alive.
Evening Block — 30 min (compound interest)
  • Engage on 10 posts in your niche on X. Real comments, not emoji spam. Ask questions. Add perspective. Be the person people start recognizing.
  • Log the day. What did you post? Who did you talk to? Any leads? Track in your Press Start dashboard. What gets measured gets done.
  • Queue tomorrow's video topic from the playbook so you wake up knowing exactly what to record.
Non-negotiable: No building new tools during selling hours. The Codex has 90+ tools. The product is done enough. If you feel the urge to build, channel it into a Soul Map for a real person instead.

Weekly Cadence

Each day has a content theme so you never wonder "what do I post about?"

Monday

Philosophy Drop. One consciousness reframe. Text post + video. "What if free will is a feature, not a bug?"

Tuesday

Tool Spotlight. Show one of your 90+ tools in action. Screen record or quick walkthrough. Make people say "wait, you built that?"

Wednesday

Soul Map Day. Share a public/demo decode. Show the visual, explain one layer. This is your product in motion.

Thursday

Founder Story. Raw. Real. Something from the build, the investigation, motherhood, the hustle. This is what makes people follow YOU not just a brand.

Friday

Community Call. Ask a question. Run a poll. Invite people into the Sparkverse. Feature a member. Make it feel like something you want to be part of.

Saturday

Behind the Code. Show the GitHub, the late-night builds, the process. Devs and creators love this. Different audience, same ecosystem.

Sunday

Rest + Reflect. Optional post. If you do, make it soft. Gratitude, a lesson from the week, a quiet thought. Or just be with Devon and Mason.

Weekly Milestones

  • 7 videos posted (one per day Mon-Sun or Mon-Sat)
  • 1 Soul Map completed or demoed
  • 15+ outbound conversations (DMs, comments, replies)
  • 1 Sparkverse community post that invites engagement
  • Sunday evening: review what worked, set next week's video topics

Rules of Engagement

Print these. Tape them to your wall. Read them when you want to build tool #91 instead of sending that DM.

01

Sell before you build.

If it doesn't have a buyer waiting, it doesn't get built. Every new tool or feature must respond to a real request or proven need. The Codex is deep enough. Now sell what's in it.

02

One video a day. No exceptions for 90 days.

Not polished. Not perfect. Just posted. The algorithm rewards consistency. Your face, your voice, your story — that's the product people buy first.

03

Conversations over funnels.

DMs convert. Automated sequences don't — not at this stage. Talk to people like humans. Ask questions. Listen. Then invite them in when it's natural.

04

The Soul Map is your lead product. Treat it like one.

Stop treating Soul Maps like a side offering. Every demo is content. Every delivery is a testimonial opportunity. Every decode is a reason someone follows you.

05

Ice box the token. Ice box the NFTs. Ice box the motorhome.

$SPRK, NFT Soul Maps, the Mobile Spark Station — all beautiful visions. All distractions at pre-revenue. Write them on a 2028 board and protect your focus.

06

Log everything. Every day.

Use Press Start. Use a notebook. Use a napkin. Track who you talked to, what you posted, what responses you got, and what money came in.

07

Be the face. Always.

People don't buy platforms from strangers. Your story — the murder investigation, the solo founder grind, the consciousness philosophy, motherhood — is your unfair advantage.

08

Don't inflate. Ever.

17 free members is 17 free members. Own the real number. Build from the real number. People respect founders who tell the truth about where they are.

09

Revenue first. Fundraise second.

Even $330/month in recurring revenue changes the fundraise conversation completely. Investors fund traction, not potential. Every dollar of revenue is worth more than every slide in the deck.

10

Right here. Right now.

Not tomorrow. Not after the next tool. Not after the perfect launch. The product is built. The story is real. The only thing between here and there is daily action. Press start.
